Cellulose nitrate (pyroxylin)

A cellulose ester, dissolves in a mixture of ketone
and alcohol solvents. It is applied as a transparent 
gel and solidifies because of solvent evaporation 
within 30 minutes in room temperature of about 20C.
A small quantity of adhesive is added to the edges of 
the broken pieces to be attached. The pieces are then 
held together until the adhesive dries.
Easily reversible and resistant to humidity, weak acids and 
atmospheric pollution its main disadvantages are its 
relatevily weak adhesive power and the fact thet it can
become yellow and brittle if exposed to sunlight for long
periods of time.
